Overview
Parallel parking
Park parallel to the curb, signalling to the side you're parking (usually right; left is valid on a one-way). Find a space about 1.5× your car's length. Slow, controlled, and within 30 cm of the curb at the end.
MTO Handbook: find a space "about one and one-half times longer than your vehicle."
Step 1 — Signal & Check
Signal to the side you'll park
Signal in advance to the direction you'll park — generally right, but left is valid on a one-way street. Signalling early tells traffic behind what you're doing. Check your mirror, then your blind spot on that side before moving into position.
MTO Handbook: "Check the traffic beside and behind and signal your wish to pull over and stop."
Step 2 — Position
Line up beside the front car, ~1 m out
Line your vehicle up beside the car you'll park behind, about a metre away, roughly parallel. This spacing sets up the whole maneuver — too close or too far throws off your angle.
MTO Handbook: "Drive alongside... leaving about a metre between the vehicles."
Step 3 — Reverse to Pivot
Reverse straight, then wheel full right
Shoulder check, then reverse straight back until your rear wheel / C-pillar pivot point is behind the car you're parking behind. At that point, while still moving slowly, turn the steering fully to the right.
MTO Handbook: "Before backing up, look all around the vehicle and check your mirrors and both blind spots... turning the steering wheel toward the curb."
Step 4 — 45° Reference
At 45° — read the passenger mirror
When you reach about 45° to the car and curb, look in your passenger mirror — the rear of your car will appear close to the curb. That's your reference point to make the next move.
Reference points beat guessing. The 45° angle plus the mirror view tell you exactly when to change your steering.
Step 5 — Bring the Front In
Wheel full left, watch both references
On that reference, while moving slowly, turn the steering all the way to the left. Use your rearview to judge distance to anything behind you, and the passenger mirror to confirm when you're parallel to the curb.
MTO Handbook: "Turn your steering wheel fully towards the road and align yourself with the curb."
Step 6 — Straighten Wheels
Right 1.5 turns to straighten
Once parallel, turn the steering wheel to the right about 1.5 turns to bring your wheels straight. Check you're centred in the bay and within 30 cm of the curb — small forward/back adjustments are fine.
MTO Handbook: "not more than about 30 centimetres away from it." Finishing beyond 30 cm is a minor error.
Step 7 — Secure
Park and set the brake
Put the vehicle in Park (or first/reverse for manual) and apply the parking brake. Before opening your door, check mirrors and shoulder check for cyclists and traffic.
MTO Handbook: "set the parking brake and move the gear selector into park... Check traffic before opening your door."
What Fails
Automatic fail: any contact
Touching the curb hard, hitting another vehicle, or striking a cone is a critical error. Slow, deliberate steering on your reference points is how you avoid it — speed is what causes contact.
Contact with an object or vehicle is an automatic fail on the road test. Keep every movement slow and controlled.
Leaving
Pulling out safely
To leave: release the brake, signal left, and shoulder check for traffic and cyclists before pulling out. Move out only when the way is clear.
MTO Handbook: when pulling out, "Release the parking brake... turn on your left-turn signal" and check before moving.
