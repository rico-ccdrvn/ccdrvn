Overview
Left turn on a solid green
Solid green = NOT protected
A solid green light is unprotected — you must yield to oncoming traffic and to pedestrians before turning. Begin and end the turn in the far-left lane of your direction.
MTO Handbook: "you can still turn left when the light is green, but only when the way is clear of traffic and pedestrians."
Step 1 — Signal & Lane
Signal early, move to the far-left lane
Signal left well before the intersection and move into the far-left lane of your direction (or the marked left-turn lane). Approach at a controlled speed.
MTO Handbook: "always begin and end a left turn in the far left lane in your direction."
Step 2 — Enter
Enter the intersection — wheels straight
Keep wheels straight + watch the light
If you're first in line, pull forward into the intersection and wait with your wheels straight. While you wait, keep watching the traffic light — it tells you when your gap is coming.
Straight wheels matter: if you're rear-ended, you won't be pushed into oncoming traffic. Only enter if you'll be able to clear the intersection.
Step 3 — Yield Oncoming
Yield to oncoming traffic
Oncoming has right-of-way
Wait for a safe gap in oncoming traffic. Oncoming vehicles going straight or turning right have the right-of-way — you yield to all of them before committing to the turn.
Don't creep your wheels left while waiting. Judge the gap on the oncoming vehicle's speed and distance, not just its distance.
Step 4 — Yield Pedestrians
Yield to pedestrians
Pedestrians cross first
Yield to pedestrians crossing the road you're turning into — they have the right-of-way. Let them fully clear your path before you proceed.
MTO Handbook: "when turning left you must yield the right-of-way to pedestrians crossing the intersection."
Step 5 — Complete the Turn
Turn in a smooth arc
When oncoming traffic and pedestrians are clear, turn in a smooth arc into the lane just right of the centre line. Then move into the curb lane when you safely can.
MTO Handbook: "turn from the lane closest to the centre line to the lane right of the centre line, following a smooth arc."
Two Setups
Standard lane vs. dedicated turn lane
4-lane: turn from left lane
With left-turn lane
On a standard multi-lane road, turn from the left lane of your direction (nearest the centre line). Where a dedicated left-turn lane is marked, turn from that lane and hold it through the turn.
MTO Handbook: "at an intersection where left-turn lanes are marked, make your turn from the marked lane."
When the Light Turns Yellow
Yellow — clear the intersection
Vacate as soon as it's safe
If you're waiting in the intersection and the light turns yellow, that's your cue. Oncoming traffic is now stopping — complete your turn and clear the intersection immediately, as soon as it's safe.
MTO Handbook: "if the light turns red when you are in the intersection, complete your turn when it is safe." Never sit in the intersection through the red.
