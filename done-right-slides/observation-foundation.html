CC Driving Observation — Done Right 1 / 10
Why It Matters
Observation is the #1 thing scored
More road-test marks are lost on observation than any other category. The examiner can only credit what they can see you do — so observation must be visible, not just intended.
Most observation marks aren't lost because the student didn't look — they're lost because the look wasn't visible enough to count.
The Core Distinction
A glance is not a check
Shifting only your eyes doesn't register as observation. A real check is a visible head turn — the movement is what makes it count.
If someone in the passenger seat couldn't tell you checked, neither can the examiner. The head turn is the signal.
What Gets Measured
The camera sees what the examiner sees
My Lesson Review tracks your head movement the same way an examiner watches it — the cabin camera measures whether your head actually turned for each check.
This is why the report can tell the difference between a real shoulder check and an eyes-only glance: it's measuring head angle, just like the examiner is watching for it.
Observation Type 1
Mirror checks — a visible flick
Mirror checks are quicker than shoulder checks — a deliberate flick of the eyes up to the rearview or across to a side mirror. Done before braking, before signalling, before any move.
Even mirror checks should be visible enough to read as a deliberate look, not a vague drift. A tiny head movement toward the mirror helps it register.
Observation Type 2
The shoulder check — full head turn
The shoulder check is a full head turn — chin toward the shoulder. It's the only way to clear the blind spot mirrors can't see, and the big head movement is unmistakable to the examiner.
Required before every lane change, merge, and turn. Eyes alone never clear a blind spot — the head has to come around.
The Rhythm
One second each — inside a 3-second signal
Give each check a full second: rearview, side mirror, then chin to shoulder — one second each. The signal stays on 3 seconds before you act, so all three checks fit inside that window.
Counting "one… two… three" as you check builds the rhythm. Rushing the three into half a second is what makes them read as eyes-only glances.
The Pattern
Scan: left — right — left
At every intersection, scan left, then right, then left again before proceeding. Left comes first and last because the nearest traffic reaches you from the left.
In motion, the equivalent is keeping your eyes moving — ahead, mirrors, and around — rather than fixing on one point. Examiners watch for active, moving eyes.
The Triggers
When a check is required
  • ✓
    Before signalling — mirror check first, so you know what's around you
  • ✓
    Before braking or slowing — rearview, to know who's behind
  • ✓
    Before every lane change — mirror + signal + shoulder check
  • ✓
    Before every turn — mirrors + shoulder check toward the turn
  • ✓
    At every intersection — left-right-left, even on a green
  • ✓
    Pulling from a curb or parking spot — mirror + shoulder check before moving
Observation isn't occasional — it's triggered by specific actions. Any time you change speed, direction, or position, a check comes first.
Build the habit so the check happens automatically before the action — examiners notice when observation comes after the move instead of before it.
What Loses Marks
Common observation errors
  • ✕
    Eyes only, no head turn — the most common one; reads as no check at all
  • ✕
    Skipping the shoulder check — relying on mirrors alone leaves the blind spot unchecked
  • ✕
    Checking too late — observing after starting the move instead of before
  • ✕
    Not re-checking — conditions change; one check at the start isn't enough
  • ✕
    Staring / fixating — locking eyes on one point instead of keeping them moving
Nearly every observation error comes down to a check that was invisible, skipped, late, or not repeated. Fixing those four covers most of the marks lost in this category.
If your lesson review flags a missed check, this is usually why — the look happened with the eyes but not the head, so it didn't register.
