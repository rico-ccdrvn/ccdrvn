Overview
Right turn at a stop sign
✓ Near lane (tight)
✕ Wide = error
Your car approaches in the right (curb) lane — the bottom lane. You turn right into the near lane of the cross street. Never swing wide into the far lane.
MTO Handbook: "always begin and end a right turn close to the right side of the road."
Step 1 — Signal
Right signal — 3 sec before the stop
← min. 3 seconds of signal → stop
Right signal on while still rolling — at least 3 seconds before reaching the stop sign. Stay in the right (curb) lane, close to the curb as you slow.
Late signal is an immediate road test error.
Step 2 — Shoulder Check
Right shoulder check — before you stop
Blind spot — curb side ↓
While still moving, chin to right shoulder. Clear the curb-side blind spot — looking for cyclists or scooters before you brake to stop.
Do this before stopping. If someone is there, slow earlier to give them space.
Step 3 — Full Stop
Complete stop — behind the line
✓ Nose at line — wheels still
✕ Rolling stop = immediate error
VIEW BLOCKED?
Creep forward slowly — don't enter the cross lane.
✓Clear left → proceed, no 2nd stop
!Traffic left → stop, don't block crosswalk
Wheels fully stopped, nose at the stop line. Any forward rolling — even slow — is an immediate road test error.
Two-stage stop: if your view left is blocked (parked cars, hedge, snowbank), creep slowly forward for a better look — see decision flow.
Step 4a — Mirrors
Rearview mirror at the stop
Quick glance to the rearview mirror. Confirm the car behind has stopped safely before you ease into the turn.
About 1 second. You need to know whether you have room behind you to ease forward.
Step 4b — Mirrors
Right side mirror
Cyclist approaching curb side
After the rearview, check the right side mirror. Looking for cyclists approaching from behind along the right curb.
The side mirror covers the approach zone but can't see right beside you — the shoulder check clears that.
Step 5 — Shoulder Check
Right shoulder check — at the stop
May have caught up while stopped
Chin to right shoulder again. A cyclist may have pulled up beside you while you were stationary. Most commonly missed check on the road test.
You checked before stopping — always check again at the stop before moving.
Step 6 — Clear & Turn
Check left — turn into the near lane
Hood centre → near lane centre
Check left — confirm no cross traffic. When clear, steer right. Aim the centre of your hood at the centre of the near lane. Tight corner, near lane only.
MTO Handbook: "complete your turn into the right-hand lane of the road you are entering."
