CC Driving
Signalling
1 / 8
Introduction
Signal every change of direction
Every change of direction or road position — turn, lane change, pulling out, pulling over — gets a signal, turned on at least 3 seconds before you act. A signal is a message to other road users, not a formality.
Signal before mirrors and shoulder check — while you check, others are already seeing your intention.
Step-by-Step
Signal, then check, then act
Signal goes on first — then rearview mirror, side mirror, shoulder check — then steer. Every check happens after the signal is already on.
The signal goes on FIRST so others have notice while you complete your checks. Checking mirrors before signalling is a common error.
Intersections
Signal about 30 m before a turn
Signal roughly 30 m before the turn — not so late you are already turning, and not blocks ahead where it confuses other drivers. Never turn without a signal — an unsignalled turn is a MAJOR error on the test, the most serious signalling mistake.
30 m is roughly 3–4 car lengths before the stop line. When in doubt, signal a little earlier rather than later.
Lane Changes
Signal before every lane change
Signal before you begin moving over, and plan the change early and smoothly. A late signal after you have started drifting is still an error on the test.
Shoulder-check the blind spot AFTER the signal is already on — not before. Signal → mirrors → shoulder → steer.
Curb Stops
Signal before you pull out or pull over
Signal before pulling away from the curb (signal left) and signal right before moving toward the curb to stop. The same rule applies for roadside stops anywhere along the road.
Both signals are required. Forgetting to signal when pulling away from the curb at the start of a test is a common first-minute error.
Passing
Signal out, signal back
Signal left before moving out of your lane to pass, then signal right before returning to your lane. Both signals are required — forgetting the return signal is a common oversight.
Complete your shoulder check (left before moving out, right before returning) in the full Signal → Mirror → Shoulder → Steer sequence each time.
Maneuvers
Signal on three-point turns and after an emergency stop
On a three-point turn, signal the direction of your initial travel before you begin. After an emergency stop, signal before rejoining traffic — treat it the same as pulling away from a curb.
Three-point turn: signal left for your first forward movement. Emergency stop restart: full Signal → Mirror → Shoulder → Steer sequence before pulling out.
