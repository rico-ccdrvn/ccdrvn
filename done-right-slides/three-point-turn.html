Overview
The 3-point turn
The turn-of-choice when a road is too narrow for a U-turn. You turn the wheel and move the car three times to reverse direction. Turn the wheel all the way for each step — or three steps won't be enough — and keep looking for traffic throughout.
Zutobi / MTO: start from the far right side to get maximum use of the road. Only do it when you have plenty of room both ways and won't disrupt traffic.
Step 1
Pull over to the right, wait for clear
Signal and pull over to the far right side of the road. Stay there until traffic is clear and you have plenty of room in both directions. Starting from the far right gives you the most road to work with.
Zutobi: start from the far right side of the road to get as much use of the road as possible as you maneuver.
Step 2
Wheel all the way left, cross the road
After waiting for a safe gap, signal left and turn the steering wheel all the way to the left. Turning it fully gives you the maximum change in direction. Drive left across the road all the way to the curb on the opposite side.
Zutobi: turning the wheel all the way enables you to get the maximum change of direction from the turn.
Step 3
Wheel all the way right, reverse
Signal right, shift to reverse, and do a full 360° check — physically look over your right shoulder. Turn the steering wheel all the way to the right and reverse slowly until you reach the curb behind you.
Zutobi: once the wheel is all the way to the right, reverse until you reach the curb. Keep checking traffic — the maneuver takes several seconds.
Step 4
Wheel left, straighten into your lane
You're now facing the wrong side of the road. Signal left, shift to drive, check for traffic, and turn the wheel to the left once more — straighten up as you pull forward into the correct lane, centring on the lane you're entering.
Zutobi: turn the wheel once more to the left and straighten your vehicle by driving forward into the correct lane.
Common Mistakes
What loses marks
The five common errors: not signalling, not checking your surroundings, starting off-centre instead of the far right, rushing, and misjudging distances. Slow control, full steering, and constant observation fix all five.
Zutobi: turn the wheels all the way for each step, take your time, and continuously look for traffic in your mirrors.
